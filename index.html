  • Flight Delay Analysis and Prediction Project

    • This project explores factors contributing to flight delays in the U.S. and develops predictive models to improve airline scheduling and operational efficiency. Using a dataset of over 100 million records, the analysis identifies key predictors such as flight schedules, airport characteristics, and weather conditions. Machine learning models, including logistic regression, decision trees, and random forests, are employed to forecast delays, with Bayesian methods further enhancing accuracy. The project aims to provide data-driven insights to minimize disruptions, reduce costs, and improve overall passenger experience.

  • Exploring the Relationship Between Public Transport Ridership and Gas Price

    • This project investigates the relationship between gas prices and public transportation ridership over a three-year period. By integrating daily ridership data with gas price trends, the analysis explores how fuel costs impact commuting behavior. Statistical modeling techniques, including linear regression and Linear Mixed Models, are applied to assess trends and correlations. The project also features an interactive R Shiny app with a Leaflet map, offering dynamic visualizations to aid transportation planners in optimizing services and policies.

  • Mapping and Spatial Analyzing of Crime in Twin Cities

    • This project examines eight years of crime data across 17 neighborhoods to identify spatial and demographic crime patterns. Using exploratory data analysis and statistical modeling, the study employs Lasso regression for predictor selection and Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) to enhance prediction accuracy. Moran’s I-test is used to detect spatial correlations in residuals, ensuring robust model performance. Findings contribute to a deeper understanding of crime distribution and potential policy interventions.
